# HOLD App - Website Design Document

This document outlines the design and theme for the HOLD app promotional website. The website's primary goal is to explain what the app does and encourage users to download it.

## 1. Concept & Vibe
**HOLD** is a grounding/mental wellness app designed to help users when their "mind gets too loud." The website should reflect this purpose by feeling:
- **Calm & Grounded:** Using deep, natural colors.
- **Minimalist & Clean:** Avoiding clutter or overwhelming elements, keeping the focus on the core message.
- **Empathetic & Safe:** The language and visuals should feel welcoming and reassuring.

## 2. Color Palette
The website will inherit the exact color palette used in the mobile app to maintain brand consistency:

| Color Use | Hex Code | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Background** | `#0a2e26` | Deep forest green. Sets a calm, natural, and immersive tone. |
| **Primary Text (Headings)** | `#f4ebd8` | Cream / off-white. Soft contrast against the dark background, easy on the eyes. |
| **Secondary Text (Body)** | `#87a89f` | Muted sage green. Used for subtitles, descriptions, and supporting text. |
| **Button / Call to Action** | `#1e483e` | Lighter forest green. Used for the download button to make it interactive while blending with the theme. |

## 3. Typography
- **Headings (H1, H2, H3):** A modern, clean, sans-serif font (e.g., *Inter*, *Outfit*, or *SF Pro Display*). Headings should be bold to convey strength and stability.
- **Body Text:** A highly readable sans-serif (e.g., *Inter*, *Roboto*). Keep line-height generous (around `1.6`) to make reading effortless and calming.

## 4. Website Structure (Single Page Landing Page)

### Section 1: Hero Area (Above the fold)
- **Headline:** Something impactful. *e.g., "For when your mind gets too loud."*
- **Sub-headline:** A brief explanation. *e.g., "Hold is a simple grounding tool. Press both volume buttons to start a mental reset."*
- **Call to Action (CTA):** A prominent download button (e.g., "Download for Android").
- **Visual:** A minimal, stylized graphic or a clean mockup of the app screen.

### Section 2: How It Works
- **Step 1:** Feel overwhelmed.
- **Step 2:** Press and hold both volume buttons.
- **Step 3:** Experience a guided, calming reset.

### Section 3: Final Call to Action (Footer Area)
- **Headline:** "Ready to find your quiet?"
- **CTA:** Another Download Button.
- **Footer Links:** Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, Contact (minimalist footer).

## 5. UI/UX Elements
- **Buttons:** Rounded corners (e.g., `border-radius: 30px`), keeping the edges soft and non-threatening.
- **Hover Effects:** Subtle and smooth. The download button could slightly lighten or lift on hover to indicate interactivity without being jarring.
- **Spacing:** Use generous padding and margins. White space (or "dark space" in this case) is crucial for a calming effect. Do not cram text together.

## 6. Imagery and Assets
- Avoid overly busy stock photos.
- If using imagery, use soft, abstract shapes, subtle gradients (using the brand colors), or high-quality, minimalist nature photography (e.g., a quiet forest, calm water) that matches the deep green tone.
- The app logo should be placed cleanly in the top navigation or hero section.
